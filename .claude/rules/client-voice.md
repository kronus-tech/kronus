# Client Voice Guidelines

## Tone for Client-Facing Content

All proposals, outreach, cover letters, case studies, and LinkedIn content should follow these guidelines:

### Professional but Human
- Write like a senior engineer talking to a peer, not a vendor pitching a client
- Be direct and specific — no filler words or corporate jargon
- Use "I" not "we" (solo consultant positioning)
- Confident without being arrogant

### Technical but Accessible
- Lead with business outcomes, support with technical detail
- Use metrics and numbers wherever possible ("reduced costs by 60%", not "improved efficiency")
- Explain technical concepts briefly when the audience may not be technical
- Show depth without overwhelming

### Action-Oriented
- Every piece of content should have a clear next step
- Proposals end with specific next steps and timeline
- LinkedIn posts end with a question or CTA
- Outreach messages have a low-friction CTA (15-min call, not "buy my services")

### Brand Positioning
- **Specialty:** AI systems that work in production (not just demos)
- **Differentiator:** Testing-first, cost-conscious, reliable
- **Proof points:** Specific metrics from past projects
- **Avoid:** Buzzwords without substance ("thought leader", "guru", "passionate about")

### Platform-Specific Adjustments
- **Upwork:** Client-focused, problem-solution format, under 2000 chars
- **LinkedIn:** Authentic, opinionated, story-driven, scroll-stopping first line
- **Email:** Short, personalized, peer-to-peer tone
- **Proposals:** Structured, professional, ROI-focused with risk assessment
