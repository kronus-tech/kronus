# Using Kronus for Legal Work

Kronus drafts documents, tracks cases and clients, researches precedents, and helps you stay organized.

## 5 Things to Try

### 1. Draft a document
> "Draft a non-disclosure agreement for [scenario]. Jurisdiction: [state/country]."

Kronus generates a professional draft for your review. It can adapt to different templates and jurisdictions.

### 2. Review a contract
> "Review this contract and flag: potential risks, missing clauses, ambiguous language, and unusual terms."

Paste the text or send a document. Kronus gives a structured review with specific recommendations.

### 3. Research case law
> "Research relevant case law on [topic] in [jurisdiction]. Focus on [specific angle]."

Kronus searches its knowledge base and can help you structure findings into a legal memorandum.

### 4. Track clients and deadlines
> "Create a client matter tracker with columns for: client, matter, status, next deadline, and notes."

Kronus builds a tracker you can keep updated. It saves as a searchable note in your knowledge base.

### 5. Create reusable templates
> "Create a template for client engagement letters that includes: scope of work, fee structure, and terms."

Templates are saved as notes. Ask Kronus to "fill in the engagement letter template for [new client]" next time.

## Your Knowledge Base

During setup, Kronus created these folders for you:
- **Cases/** — active and past cases
- **Clients/** — client information and matter tracking
- **Legal-Research/** — research notes and case law summaries
- **Templates/** — document templates
- **Archive/** — completed matters

## Privacy Note

Kronus runs entirely on your machine. Client data is never sent to third-party servers beyond the AI processing itself. No data is stored externally.
