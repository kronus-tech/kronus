# Using Kronus for Research

Kronus organizes your papers, tracks your literature reviews, finds connections across your work, and helps you write.

## 5 Things to Try

### 1. Organize your papers
> "Help me categorize my recent papers by topic and create a reading tracker"

Kronus creates a structured tracker with status, key findings, and cross-references.

### 2. Literature review
> "Create a literature review outline for [your topic]. I need to cover [key areas]."

It builds a structured outline, suggests sections, and can help fill in each part with your notes.

### 3. Find connections
> "What themes connect my research notes on [topic A] and [topic B]?"

The knowledge graph shows how your notes relate. Kronus can find patterns you might miss.

### 4. Summarize a paper
> "Summarize this paper and extract: key findings, methodology, limitations, and how it relates to my work on [topic]."

Send a PDF or paste text. Kronus gives a structured summary you can save as a note.

### 5. Draft sections
> "Help me draft the methodology section for my paper on [topic]. My approach uses [methods]."

Kronus writes a draft in academic style, which you can review and revise.

## Your Knowledge Base

During setup, Kronus created these folders for you:
- **Papers/** — papers you're reading or have read
- **Research-Notes/** — your research observations and ideas
- **Literature-Reviews/** — review documents and outlines
- **Grants/** — grant applications and funding
- **Archive/** — completed work

## Tips

- Use `[[note name]]` in your notes to link them together — the knowledge graph builds automatically
- Ask Kronus to "search my notes for [topic]" to find relevant past work
- The knowledge graph at localhost:4242 shows how all your research connects
