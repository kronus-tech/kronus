# Using Kronus for Studying

Kronus explains concepts, helps with assignments, organizes your notes, and creates study materials — like having a tutor available 24/7.

## 5 Things to Try

### 1. Understand a concept
> "Explain [concept] in simple terms with examples. I'm studying [course/subject]."

Ask follow-up questions until it clicks. Try: "Explain it a different way" or "Give me a real-world example."

### 2. Get help with assignments
> "Help me outline my essay on [topic]. The requirements are: [length, format, key points to cover]."

Kronus helps you plan and structure — you do the writing. It can review drafts too.

### 3. Create study materials
> "Create flashcards for [topic/chapter]. Include definitions, key concepts, and example problems."

Or: "Create 20 practice questions for my [subject] exam on [topics]."

### 4. Organize your notes
> "Help me organize my notes for [course]. Group them by topic and highlight the key themes."

Kronus can restructure messy notes into clean, searchable documents.

### 5. Build a study plan
> "Create a study schedule for my [exam] on [date]. I need to cover: [topics]. I have [hours] per day."

Structured plan with spaced repetition and review sessions.

## Your Knowledge Base

- **Courses/** — course information and syllabi
- **Assignments/** — current and past assignments
- **Study-Notes/** — lecture notes, summaries, key concepts
- **Resources/** — textbooks, links, reference material
- **Archive/** — completed courses

## Tips

- Ask Kronus to "quiz me on [topic]" before exams
- Use "Am I right that [your understanding]?" to check your thinking
- The knowledge graph shows how topics connect across courses
