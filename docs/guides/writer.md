# Using Kronus for Writing

Kronus helps with research, outlining, drafting, editing, and managing your content calendar — without overwriting your voice.

## 5 Things to Try

### 1. Generate ideas
> "Generate 10 content ideas about [niche/topic]. Target audience: [who]. Mix of [types: articles, posts, guides]."

Kronus suggests ideas with angles, hooks, and potential headlines.

### 2. Outline a piece
> "Create a detailed outline for an article about [topic]. Target: [audience]. Tone: [casual/professional/academic]. Length: [words]."

Structured outline with sections, key points, and suggested examples.

### 3. Research a topic
> "Research [topic] and give me 5 key angles, supporting data, and expert perspectives I could reference."

Quick research summary to inform your writing.

### 4. Edit for clarity
> "Review this draft for: clarity, flow, engagement, and grammar. Keep my voice — just improve the structure."

Paste your draft. Kronus suggests specific improvements without rewriting your style.

### 5. Manage your calendar
> "What am I working on this week? What's due? What should I start next?"

Kronus checks your content calendar note and gives you a status update.

## Your Knowledge Base

- **Drafts/** — work in progress
- **Published/** — completed and published pieces
- **Research/** — research notes, style guides, reference material
- **Ideas/** — content ideas and inspiration
- **Archive/** — old drafts and retired content
